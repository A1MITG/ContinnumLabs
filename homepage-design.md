# Continuum Labs – Homepage Design Specification

**Brand Name:** Continuum Labs  
**Tagline:** Built across time. Shipped monthly.

---

## 1️⃣ HERO SECTION

### Purpose
Immediate orientation. The visitor should understand what Continuum Labs is within 5 seconds, without persuasion or hype.

### Layout Concept
- **Vertical center alignment** on initial viewport
- **Generous vertical padding** (120px–160px top/bottom)
- **Maximum width: 1200px**, center-aligned
- **Minimal distractions** – no background imagery, just subtle depth

### Content Hierarchy

```
[LOGO/WORDMARK]
Small, top-left, understated

                    CONTINUUM LABS
               [Large, bold, 64–80px]
            Built across time. Shipped monthly.
                  [Lighter, 18–20px]

        We build one thoughtful app every month.
         Long-term thinking. Consistent execution.
                  [Body, 16–18px, muted]

           [Suggest the Next App]    [See What We've Built]
            [Primary button]         [Secondary/ghost button]
```

### Design Direction
- **Typography dominates** – no hero image
- **Background:** Soft off-white with **subtle radial gradient** from center (hint of cosmic blue at 2–3% opacity)
- **Optional motif:** Thin horizontal line threading across the page, suggesting continuity/timeline
- **Spacing:** Minimum 40px between elements
- **Buttons:**
  - Primary: Deep indigo background, white text, subtle shadow
  - Secondary: Outlined in slate, transparent fill

### Microcopy

**Headline:** Continuum Labs  
**Tagline:** Built across time. Shipped monthly.  
**Subhead:** We build one thoughtful app every month. Long-term thinking. Consistent execution.  
**Primary CTA:** Suggest the Next App  
**Secondary CTA:** See What We've Built

### UX Rationale
- **No fluff** – visitors know immediately what this is
- **Dual CTAs** serve different user intents: participate vs. explore
- **Restraint signals credibility** – no overpromising

## 2️⃣ MANIFESTO SECTION

### Purpose
Communicate the "why" behind Continuum Labs. This is not marketing – it's a declaration of intent.

### Layout Concept
- **Full-width section** with contained text block
- **Maximum text width: 720px** (optimal reading length)
- **Large line-height** (1.7–1.9) for readability and breathing room
- **Section padding:** 160px top/bottom

### Content Hierarchy

```
                          Why This Exists
                          [Section label, 14px, uppercase, tracked]

      Most platforms are built for short-term wins.
      Continuum Labs is different.

      We build tools that compound over time.
      Tools designed for people who think in decades, not quarters.

      Every month, we ship one app.
      Every app is a bet on a better way to work, think, or create.

      No hype. No pivots. Just consistent execution.
      [18–22px body text, dark charcoal, generous spacing]
```

### Design Direction
- **Background:** Midnight slate (near-black)
- **Text color:** Soft off-white or light gray
- **Typography:** Slightly heavier weight (500) for emphasis
- **Alignment:** Center-aligned text block
- **No decoration** – let the words carry weight

### Microcopy

**Section Label:** Why This Exists

**Body:**
> Most platforms are built for short-term wins. Continuum Labs is different.
>
> We build tools that compound over time. Tools designed for people who think in decades, not quarters.
>
> Every month, we ship one app. Every app is a bet on a better way to work, think, or create.
>
> No hype. No pivots. Just consistent execution.

### UX Rationale
- **Editorial tone** – feels like reading a founder's note, not ad copy
- **Dark section creates visual break** and signals depth
- **Short paragraphs maintain rhythm** and prevent wall-of-text

### Design Direction
- **Background:** Soft off-white
- **Cards:**
  - Border: 1px solid light gray
  - Padding: 48px
  - Background: Pure white
  - Subtle shadow on hover (2px depth)

## 4️⃣ SUBTLE GENERATIONAL CUE

### Purpose
Add depth and intrigue without explicit explanation. Visitors should sense there's a deeper story without it being spelled out.

### Layout Concept
- **Horizontal split or mirrored layout**
- **Minimal text, maximum structural contrast**
- **Section padding:** 100px top/bottom

### Content Hierarchy

```
        ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
        │                                          │
        │     Foundation              Momentum     │
        │                                          │
        │  [Serif/grounded]        [Sans/forward] │
        │  Architecture            Iteration       │
        │  Strategy                Execution        │
        │  Vision                  Velocity         │
        │                                          │
        └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Alternative approach – Visual Duality:**
- Two columns with contrasting typography
- Left: Slightly heavier, grounded (represents long-term foundation)
- Right: Lighter, dynamic (represents forward momentum)
- Joined by a subtle connecting element (line, gradient, or shared border)

### Design Direction
- **Background:** Deep indigo with subtle texture or gradient
- **Text:** Off-white
- **Visual treatment:**
  - Dual perspectives suggested through typography contrast
  - Subtle connecting element (thin line, gradient fade between sections)
  - No human imagery, no explicit family reference
- **Layout:**
  - 50/50 split or slightly asymmetrical (45/55)
  - Vertical divider line in accent color (electric violet, low opacity)

### Microcopy

**Left Column (Foundation):**
- Architecture
- Strategy  
- Vision

**Right Column (Momentum):**
- Iteration
- Execution
- Velocity

**Optional subtle headline:** "Built on two timescales"

### UX Rationale
- **Implies complementary forces** without stating them
- **Creates curiosity** without demanding explanation
- **Visual balance suggests partnership** without being literal
- **Visitors who notice will appreciate the depth**; those who don't will simply see balance

## KEY DESIGN DECISIONS & RATIONALE

### 1. Typography-First Approach
**Decision:** No hero imagery, no stock photos  
**Rationale:** Words carry more weight than generic visuals. Continuum Labs is about ideas and execution – let the copy prove that.

### 2. Dark Sections as Punctuation
**Decision:** Use midnight slate/deep indigo for key message sections  
**Rationale:** Creates visual hierarchy, signals "this matters," and prevents monotony.

### 3. Restraint in Motion
**Decision:** Minimal animation – subtle fades, no parallax, no elaborate transitions  
**Rationale:** Motion should reinforce continuity (smooth, purposeful), not distract.

### 4. Dual CTAs Throughout
**Decision:** Always offer two paths – participate vs. observe  
**Rationale:** Respects different visitor intents. Some want to engage immediately; others want to learn first.

### 5. Monthly Proof Point
**Decision:** Featured app with "Built this month" indicator  
**Rationale:** Concrete proof of the "shipped monthly" promise. Execution over talk.

### 6. Generational Cue Without Explanation
**Decision:** Structural/typographic duality, no explicit mention  
**Rationale:** Adds depth for those who notice, doesn't alienate those who don't. Sophistication through subtlety.

### 7. Build-in-Public as Credibility
**Decision:** Show works-in-progress and failures, not just wins  
**Rationale:** Honesty differentiates. Most sites hide the process; showing it builds trust.
